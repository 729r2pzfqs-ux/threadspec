Formulas Used (ISO 724 / ASME B1.1)

H = (√3/2) × P — Fundamental triangle height

d₂ = d − 0.6495 × P — Pitch diameter

d₁ = d − 1.2269 × P — Minor diameter (external)

D₁ = d − 1.0825 × P — Minor diameter (internal / tap drill)

Aₛ = (π/4) × ((d₂ + d₃)/2)² — Tensile stress area, where d₃ = d₁ − H/6

Frequently Asked Questions

How is tensile stress area calculated?

Tensile stress area is calculated as As = (pi/4) × ((d2 + d3)/2)², where d2 is the pitch diameter and d3 = d1 - H/6 (d1 = d - 1.0825P is the basic minor diameter, H is the fundamental triangle height). This is per ISO 898-1. For unified inch threads, the calculator uses the ASME B1.1 formula As = 0.7854 × (D - 0.9743/n)², where n is threads per inch.

What formulas does this calculator use?

This calculator uses ISO 724 formulas for metric threads and ASME B1.1 for unified inch threads. Pitch diameter: d2 = d - 0.6495P. Minor diameter: d1 = d - 1.2269P. Thread depth: 5H/8 where H = (sqrt(3)/2) × P.
