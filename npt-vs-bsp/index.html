NPT vs BSP — Pipe Thread Comparison

NPT (National Pipe Taper) and BSP (British Standard Pipe) are the two major pipe thread standards used worldwide. They are not interchangeable despite similar nominal sizes.

StandardASME B1.20.1ISO 228 / ISO 7-1 / BS 21

Thread FormFlattened crests and rootsRounded crests and roots
Taper1:16 (tapered)BSPT: 1:16 (tapered)
BSPP: parallel
Seal MethodThread deformation + sealant (tape/dope)BSPT: thread deformation
BSPP: O-ring or washer seal
Primary UseNorth AmericaEurope, Asia, Australia, Africa
Compatible?No — different thread angles make them non-interchangeable. Adapters required.

Key Differences

Thread angle: NPT uses a 60° thread angle (same as metric/unified), while BSP uses the Whitworth 55° angle. This means the thread profiles are fundamentally different and cannot mate properly, even when the TPI happens to match.

Sealing: NPT relies on the tapered threads themselves deforming to create a seal, typically assisted by PTFE tape or pipe dope. BSPP (parallel) threads don't seal on the threads at all — they use a bonded washer or O-ring on a machined seat.

Measurement: Nominal pipe size (NPS) refers to a historical bore size, not the actual OD. A "1/2 inch NPT" fitting has an actual OD of 0.840" — the same as "1/2 inch BSP" which has an OD of 0.825". Close enough to thread together a turn or two, but not to seal.
